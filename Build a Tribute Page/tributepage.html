Dr. Norman Borlaug

This is the guy in the picture.

Timeline

  • 1918 - Born in Queens, New York City
  • 1933 - Teaches himself trigonometry, advanced algebra, infinite series, analytic geometry and differential/integral caculus at the age of 15
  • 1939 - Receives B.S. from MIT and is named a Putnam Fellow
  • 1942 - Receives Ph.D from Princeton University
  • 1942-1945 - Worked on the Manhatten Project for developing the atomic bomb
  • 1945-1950 - Teaches theoretical physics at Cornell University
  • 1950 - Joins Caltech. Explains all the things.
  • 1965 - Receives Nobel Prize for his work in quantum electrodynamics
  • 1979 - Receives National Medal of Science award
  • 1986 - Plays important role in determining the cause of the Challenger space shuttle disaster
  • 1988 - Dies in Los Angeles, California at the age of 69
